From Simple Prompts to Custom GPTs

Understanding how AI assistants really work — and how to build your own

The Basics

Prompt and Response

When you type something into ChatGPT, something simple happens: the AI reads your prompt and generates a response. That's the entire interaction.

Each conversation starts fresh. The AI has no memory of past conversations—it only knows what you've told it in the current chat.

The Problem

Wanting Repeatability

Sometimes you get a response that's exactly what you needed—helpful, well-formatted, accurate. You want that same quality every time.

But the next time you ask a similar question, the response is different. Maybe it's shorter. Maybe it uses a different format. Maybe it takes a different approach entirely.

The AI doesn't remember what worked before. Every new conversation, you're starting over from scratch.

Solution Level 1

Saving Your Prompts

Here's the first insight: if you write good instructions once, why write them every time?

Create a text file with your instructions. Save it on your computer. At the start of each new conversation, paste those instructions into the chat before asking your question.

Now you're getting more consistent responses. The AI behaves the same way each time because it's receiving the same instructions each time.

Key insight: You're essentially programming the AI's behavior with your saved prompt. The prompt is your code; the AI is your executor.

Adding Context

Files Make It Smarter

Your prompt tells the AI how to respond—the tone, format, and approach you want.

But what if you want the AI to know specific facts? Your company's FAQ. Your department's policies. Your organization's style guide.

Upload a file along with your prompt. Now the AI can reference your information, not just its general knowledge. It can answer questions about your specific documents.

Key insight: Prompt (behavior) + File (knowledge) = specialized assistant. You've now created something that acts the way you want and knows what you need it to know.

Packaging It Up

Custom GPTs

What if you didn't have to paste your prompt and upload your files every time you started a new conversation?

That's exactly what Custom GPTs are. They let you save:

  • Your instructions — the prompt that defines behavior
  • Your files — the knowledge it can reference
  • Conversation starters — helpful example questions to get started

You can share your Custom GPT with others via a simple link. They get the same consistent, specialized assistant you built.

Key insight: A Custom GPT is just your saved prompt + files, packaged for convenience. The AI underneath works exactly the same way. There's no magic—just smart packaging.
